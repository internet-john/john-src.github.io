Feature Development for Frontend Devs -- Some considerations

  • What is the biz objective?
  • How does this feature satisfy biz objective? Tech objective always services biz objective. (Consult with product managers, Biz Dev folks)
  • Who are the users? What user pain are we addressing? (Here is the time to sync up with design, product managers, customer care reps, customers themselves)
  • Are there wireframes? Specs? What is the brand we are communicating? At a scrappy startup, often times, a front end dev is also the designer. Otherwise, reach out to design team for this.
  • Where in the product can we find similar interactions and UI? Brand consistency matters.
  • What are the explicit interactions of this page? What are the implicit and explicit behavior and outcomes? Where does this fit within rest of product / interaction? It is so, so, so important to be as granular as possible here. It pays hugely to spend a lot of time here fleshing out every little detail. You can do your job with ambiguity, but you can do your job far more effectively with specificity.
  • What is the technical scope of the change? Is it purely UI? Will I need to work on the server? Who is my point of contact in which I may depend upon if working in unfamiliar territory.
  • What do the wireframes look like translated to UI? What does this UI look like broken down into components? What is the minimal state representation of the UI?
  • Are we talking to API’s? How are we handling the loading case? How are we handling caching? How are we managing latency?
  • Are we displaying data? How much? If large dataset, do we have a pagination strategy? Is there someone / somewhere else within product I can reference?
  • Is this a mobile app or website?
  • How are we managing state transitions? UI entropy can quickly get out of hand.
  • What is the acceptance criteria? If you carefully detailed the interactions, behavior, and outcomes as stated above, this is your acceptance criteria.
  • What is the rollout plan? Beta? Percentage rollout? Phased released? A/B Testing? Do we have tracking and analytics in place? Have we added sufficient test coverage end-to-end? Unit, integration, and UI?

    • tl;dr

    as a dev, your job is to translate thought into code. clear, concise, thoughts = clear, conside, code

    it's in your best interest to gather the requirements yourself, drill down, get as specific as possible, THEN implement
